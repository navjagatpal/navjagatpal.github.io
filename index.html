About Me

I'm an educator, engineer, and artist passionate about integrating science, technology, and creativity. I teach physics and computer science at Westtown School and hold degrees from Columbia and the University of Delaware.

My research includes work in photonics and electro-optic modulators. I also create metal and wire sculptures, featured on Instagram.

Teaching Philosophy

I aim to make science accessible and engaging through inclusive, real-world education that inspires curiosity and application.

Research & Projects

  • Photonics Research: Thin-film lithium niobate modulators for 1064 nm wavelengths — ResearchGate
  • The Jewel Box: Acrylic and fiber optic art installation — View Project
  • Marble Machine: A decorative wall hanging, in which a marble is lifted up by a motor and rolls along a track. — View Project
  • Laser Switch: A laser beam is aimed at a receiver circuit, and a person can turn the lights on and off by waving their hand in the path of the beam. — View Project
  • The Gadgeteer’s Heart: A small decorative piece, with a motor and two rotating brass gears; which can be either worn on clothing or hung on a wall.
  • Proximity Lights: An interactive wall hanging, containing a set of LEDs and a distance sensor. The closer a person is to the piece, the more LEDs turn on.
  • Hand Wave Levitator: An aerodynamic levitation system, using a ping-pong ball and a modified hair dryer, controlled by a distance sensor. Waving one’s hand upward causes the ball to levitate.
  • Laser Frame: A wall hanging in which a green laser is reflected and scattered through a piece of glass.
  • The Wishing Well: An art installation, made as part of a team, for the biannual art festival known as Playa Del Fuego.
